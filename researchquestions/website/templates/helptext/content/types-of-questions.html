There are 4 basic kinds of questions you can ask about a topic:

Process Questions

These are questions about the about you and your research process. Examples:

  • Where do I find sources on Moby Dick?
  • Should I focus my energies on researching the causes or effects of deforestation in the northwestern US?

While important, process questions are not research questions.

Factual Questions

These are questions that have only one correct answer. Examples:

  • Are lemmings suicidal?
  • Where is the largest population of free-roaming elk in North America?

Certainly you will ask and answer some factual questions as you work on your research project, but your guiding research questions need to be something other than factual in order for you to move beyond an informative report to an analytical research project. While undoubtedly an essential part of any research process, factual questions do not make a good basis for analytical research projects.

Evaluative Questions

These are questions that require you to use your own knowledge, experience, and values in judging an issue, event or action. An evaluative question asks whether you agree with a position, or it asks you to make a value judgment, or it asks you about your beliefs. Examples:

  • Do I believe that climate change is caused by human activity?
  • Is environmental regulation by the government good for society?

The answers to evaluative questions often have very little to do with evidence. Rather, they have to do with opinion and/or ideological belief. Since scholarly research should be analytical and account for multiple perspectives, evaluative questions do not make for good research questions.

Interpretive Questions

These are the kind of open-ended questions you should strive to ask for an analytical research project. An interpretive question, as the name suggests, is open to interpretation and thus has a range of possible answers, more than one of which may be accurate or plausible. As a result, an interpretive question is open to multiple perspectives from the outset. Interpretive questions tend to start with "Why?" "How?" or "What does it mean...?"
